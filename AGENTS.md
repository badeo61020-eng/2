# AGENTS.md

## Scope

- All work in this workspace is for EC selling by default.
- Unless the user explicitly asks otherwise, reply in Markdown format.
- The user may provide product photos, product URLs, product descriptions, and screenshots.
- Product photos and screenshots must be treated as primary evidence for LP direction.

## Core Workflow

- First clarify the product with `PMM`.
- Then choose the best LP structure.
- Then write the LP based on EC research, image judgment, review analysis, and keyword analysis.

## PMM

- `Person`: who the product is for.
- `Method`: what the product does and how it solves the problem.
- `Merit`: what benefit or future the customer gets.

## LP Structure Options

### PASBECONA

- Use this order when the user asks for PASBECONA or when no other structure is requested.
- `Problem`
- `Affinity`
- `Solution`
- `Benefit`
- `Evidence`
- `Contents`
- `Offer`
- `Narrow`
- `Action`

### Category Format

- Use this order when the user asks for category-based LP output.
- `TOP`
- `USP`
- `問題点`
- `解決策1`
- `解決策2`
- `解決策3`
- `use`
- `仕様`

## Category Format Priority Rule

- In the category format, always treat these as the top 3 priority blocks:
- `USP`
- `問題点`
- `解決策`
- Clarify these three first before expanding the rest of the LP.
- Remove repeated or very similar content across these three blocks.
- `USP` should contain only the biggest unique difference.
- `問題点` should contain only customer dissatisfaction or pain points.
- `解決策` should contain only how the product solves those problems.
- Do not repeat the same phrase, promise, or explanation across `USP`, `問題点`, and `解決策`.

## Product Judgment Rule

- Match the LP to the real product.
- Do not exaggerate weak points into core strengths.
- If a feature is weak, do not force it as the main hook.
- Move the appeal axis to what is truly strong in the product.
- Common strong axes may include:
- design
- material
- usability
- function
- atmosphere
- lifestyle fit
- durability
- gifting value
- price appeal

## Photo-Based Judgment Rule

- When the user provides product photos, inspect them before deciding the LP angle.
- Judge as much as possible from the photos:
- shape
- size impression
- color
- material impression
- premium or casual feel
- stylish, elegant, natural, simple, practical, cute, or modern feel
- likely use scenes
- visible strengths
- visible weaknesses
- likely misunderstandings
- Prioritize the value that the photo communicates most strongly.
- Do not force a main hook that the image does not support.
- Keep the LP consistent with the image impression.

## EC Sales Rule

- Write as EC sales copy, not in-store sales talk.
- In EC, customers judge mainly from photos and text.
- The LP must do both:
- attract attention
- reduce anxiety
- Always consider:
- what the customer understands from the image immediately
- what must be explained in text
- what objections must be removed
- why this product should win against competing listings

## EC Marketplace Research Rule

- When the user asks for EC research, default to Amazon and Rakuten style research.
- Compare both when useful because they reward different strengths.
- Amazon research should focus more on:
- title structure
- first impression
- bullet clarity
- conversion efficiency
- search fit
- trust signals
- reviews
- Rakuten research should focus more on:
- first view
- long LP flow
- emotional persuasion
- image-heavy storytelling
- offer presentation
- store branding
- mobile readability
- reviews
- Use research not only to decide what to write, but also what page style fits the product best:
- Amazon-like concise style
- Rakuten-like long persuasive style
- hybrid style

## Review Analysis Rule

- Review analysis is mandatory when reviews are available.
- Always check:
- repeated praise
- repeated complaints
- expectation gaps
- misunderstandings
- size or fit complaints
- material praise or complaints
- quality concerns
- emotional wording customers repeat
- reasons customers bought
- reasons customers would not buy again
- Use repeated praise in benefit and evidence sections.
- Use repeated complaints to design objection handling.
- Distinguish between:
- product problems
- explanation problems
- If complaints come from misunderstanding, improve wording and expectation control.

## Keyword Research Rule

- Keyword research is mandatory in EC research.
- Always analyze how the product is likely to be searched.
- Collect keywords from these angles:
- product name
- material
- function
- use scene
- target user
- style or impression
- pain point
- synonym
- wording variation
- short keywords
- long-tail keywords
- For Amazon, prioritize short, clear, practical search wording.
- For Rakuten, include product-name keywords plus broader and persuasive wording.
- If the product has a special material or feature, check both:
- technical term
- easier everyday explanation
- Distinguish between:
- direct keywords
- explanatory keywords
- attractive keywords
- Use keyword research to shape:
- LP headline
- title wording
- sub-copy
- bullets
- comparison wording

## EC Image and LP Link Rule

- Product images and LP copy must work together as one selling system.
- Images attract attention first.
- The LP deepens understanding and removes hesitation.
- Do not repeat the exact same message in every section.
- Decide what the images already communicate well, then use text to fill the missing parts.
- When useful, align LP blocks with image roles:
- first view for attraction
- proof sections for material, detail, comparison, and trust
- use sections for scene fit
- offer sections for price, shipping, sets, or campaign conditions

## Writing Rule

- Do not reuse fixed wording blindly across all products.
- Adjust the LP angle for each product.
- Avoid wording that creates expectation gaps.
- Do not focus only on features; describe the life improvement after use.
- The reader should feel "this is for me".
- When the user asks for more detail, increase specificity and content depth.

## Input Checklist

- target customer
- price range
- use scene
- differences versus competitors
- visual impression
- strengths
- weaknesses
- likely customer concerns
- what the image strongly communicates

## EC Research Checklist

- major competing listings on Amazon
- major competing listings on Rakuten
- likely search keywords
- product-name keywords
- feature keywords
- material keywords
- use-scene keywords
- synonym keywords
- short keywords
- long-tail keywords
- repeated selling angles
- repeated image patterns
- common title keywords
- common benefit claims
- review-based complaints
- review-based praise
- repeated review wording
- expectation gaps
- whether complaints come from product issues or explanation issues
- offer structure such as coupon, shipping, bundle, set, and discount
- possible positioning gaps
- whether the product fits Amazon-style, Rakuten-style, or hybrid style

## Output Goal

- The LP should match the real product.
- The LP should fit EC selling.
- The LP should use the strongest realistic selling angle.
- The LP should combine attraction and reassurance.
- In category-format LPs, `USP`, `問題点`, and `解決策` must be the clearest and least repetitive sections.
