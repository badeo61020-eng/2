# Amazon and Rakuten Research Theme

## Purpose

This theme is for researching EC marketplaces before writing product LPs or sales copy.

## Why This Matters

- Amazon and Rakuten are both EC platforms, but the strongest conversion patterns are not identical.
- Research should identify how the product category is currently being sold and what type of page style is most suitable.

## Amazon Research Focus

- Amazon shoppers often decide quickly.
- Research should focus on fast understanding and conversion efficiency.
- Review analysis is essential because it reveals real buying motives and dissatisfaction.

### Check These Elements

- title structure
- main image impact
- sub-image composition
- bullet point structure
- product description structure
- A+ content patterns if visible
- price positioning
- reviews and rating volume
- common complaints
- common praised benefits
- keyword targeting
- product variation handling
- review wording and customer complaints
- repeated praise themes

### What to Learn from Amazon

- which value is communicated first
- which words are repeated across winning listings
- what kind of images improve trust
- what objections are removed early
- what benefit is prioritized over feature explanation
- what customers repeatedly praise in their own words
- what complaints are caused by the product itself versus weak listing explanation

## Rakuten Research Focus

- Rakuten often rewards stronger storytelling and longer persuasive structure.
- Research should focus on first view, page flow, visual persuasion, and offer presentation.
- Review analysis should be included whenever review data is available.

### Check These Elements

- hero image and first view
- catch copy patterns
- emotional versus practical tone
- long LP flow
- section order
- use-scene explanation
- material and detail close-ups
- comparison framing
- social proof or review use
- review wording and repeated concerns
- coupon, shipping, set, and promotion presentation
- mobile readability
- store branding consistency

### What to Learn from Rakuten

- how successful stores build desire
- how they connect product function with lifestyle
- how they handle reassurance
- how they push purchase with campaign and offer framing
- what real customers repeatedly value or complain about

## Review Analysis

- Reviews are one of the best sources for LP direction.
- They reveal both visible strengths and hidden purchase friction.

### Check These Points

- repeated praise
- repeated dissatisfaction
- expectation mismatch
- misunderstood features
- material impression
- size impression
- durability concerns
- gift suitability comments
- emotional words customers use naturally
- what made customers decide to buy
- what made customers regret buying

### How to Use Review Findings

- Turn repeated praise into benefit and evidence wording.
- Turn repeated complaints into objection handling.
- If reviews show misunderstanding, improve explanation instead of forcing stronger claims.
- If reviews reveal an unexpected valued point, consider making it a key selling angle.

## Compare Amazon and Rakuten

- Amazon is often stronger for concise decision support.
- Rakuten is often stronger for persuasive long-form selling.
- A product may need one of these styles more than the other.
- Research should determine which style is more suitable:
- concise and search-oriented
- emotional and long-form
- hybrid

## Research Output

After research, summarize:

- key competing angles
- overused claims in the market
- customer anxieties
- review-based dissatisfaction
- review-based expectation gaps
- strongest visual patterns
- strongest copy patterns
- positioning gap
- recommended LP direction
- recommended image direction
- recommended EC style:
- Amazon style
- Rakuten style
- hybrid style

## Practical Rule

- Do not copy competitor wording directly.
- Learn patterns, then create a sharper positioning.
- Use research to improve clarity, differentiation, and conversion logic.
